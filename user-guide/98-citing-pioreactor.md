# Citing Pioreactor

If you use Pioreactor in a paper, thesis, preprint, poster, presentation, or course, we appreciate a citation or acknowledgement.

We do not have a formal peer-reviewed reference paper yet. For now, cite Pioreactor directly and include enough detail in your methods for someone else to understand what you used.

## Recommended citation

Pioreactor Inc. (2026). *Pioreactor: A Raspberry Pi-powered bioreactor platform for microbial growth automation*. [https://pioreactor.com](https://pioreactor.com)

```bibtex
@misc{pioreactor,
  author       = {{Pioreactor Inc.}},
  title        = {{Pioreactor: A Raspberry Pi-powered bioreactor platform for microbial growth automation}},
  year         = {2026},
  url          = {https://pioreactor.com},
  note         = {Accessed: YYYY-MM-DD}
}
```

You can also download this citation as [BibTeX](/media-assets/pioreactor-publication-assets/citation/pioreactor.bib), [RIS](/media-assets/pioreactor-publication-assets/citation/pioreactor.ris), or [plain text](/media-assets/pioreactor-publication-assets/citation/pioreactor-apa.txt).

## What to mention in your methods

Pioreactor hardware and software are used together, so one citation is enough. In your methods, add the details that matter for reproducibility:

- the Pioreactor model, such as 20 mL or 40 mL
- the Pioreactor software version

For example:

> Experiments were performed using Pioreactor 20 mL / 40 mL bioreactors from Pioreactor Inc. (Waterloo, Ontario, Canada).

## Suggested acknowledgement

If a citation does not fit the venue, you can acknowledge Pioreactor in your methods, poster, slides, or course materials.

> Microbial growth experiments were monitored and controlled using Pioreactor, a bioreactor platform from Pioreactor Inc. (Waterloo, Ontario, Canada).

For a more specific methods statement:

> Microbial growth experiments were monitored and controlled using Pioreactor 20 mL / 40 mL bioreactors.
